4Parliamentary Constituencies Bill: Presentation (Standing Order No. 57 and Order (Presentation of bills), 22 April)

Chloe Smith, supported by the Prime Minister, the Chancellor of the Exchequer, Secretary Priti Patel, Michael Gove, Secretary Robert Jenrick, Jesse Norman and Amanda Milling, presented a Bill to make provision about reports of the Boundary Commissions under the Parliamentary Constituencies Act 1986; to make provision about the number of parliamentary constituencies and other rules for the distribution of seats; and for connected purposes.

5Human Tissue

Motion made and Question proposed, That the draft Human Tissue (Permitted Material: Exceptions) (England) Regulations 2020, which were laid before this House on 25 February, be approved.—(Helen Whately.)

The Deputy Speaker announced a time limit on backbench speeches (Standing Order No. 47(1)).

Question put and agreed to.

Resolved, That the draft Human Tissue (Permitted Material: Exceptions) (England) Regulations 2020, which were laid before this House on 25 February, be approved.

7Finance Bill (Ways and Means)

Motion made and Question proposed, That (notwithstanding anything to the contrary in the practice of the House relating to the matters that may be included in Finance Bills) provision taking effect in a future year may be made amending Chapters 8 and 10 of Part 2 of the Income Tax (Earnings and Pensions) Act 2003.—(Jesse Norman.)

The Deputy Speaker announced a time limit on backbench speeches (Standing Order No. 47(1)).

In accordance with the Speaker's determination, the Deputy Speaker announced that a remote division on the Question was not required (Order of 22 April (Remote divisions (3)).

Question put and agreed to.

Ordered, That (notwithstanding anything to the contrary in the practice of the House relating to the matters that may be included in Finance Bills) provision taking effect in a future year may be made amending Chapters 8 and 10 of Part 2 of the Income Tax (Earnings and Pensions) Act 2003.

Ordered, That it be an instruction to the Committee on the Finance Bill that it have power to make provision pursuant to the foregoing Order.
