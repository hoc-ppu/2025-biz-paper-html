House of Commons
Session 2019-21

Published: Friday 31 January 2020

Early Day Motions tabled on Thursday 30 January 2020

Early Day Motions (EDMs) are motions for which no days have been fixed.

The number of signatories includes all members who have added their names in support of the Early Day Motion (EDM), including the Member in charge of the Motion.

EDMs and added names are also published on the EDM database at www.parliament.uk/edm

[R] Indicates that a relevant interest has been declared.

New EDMs

124 Closure of Loanhead Bank of Scotland branch

Tabled: 30/01/20 Signatories: 1

Owen Thompson

That this House notes with concern the proposals by Bank of Scotland to close a number of branches, including the Loanhead Bank of Scotland in Midlothian; notes that the Loanhead branch is the only bank in Loanhead and is the nearest bank for the communities of Bilston, Damhead and Roslin; considers that Bank of Scotland has not taken fully into account the growing population in these communities; and calls on Bank of Scotland to reconsider this short sighted proposal.

125 Interserve industrial action at the Foreign and Commonwealth Office

Tabled: 30/01/20 Signatories: 1

Chris Stephens [R]

That this House supports members of the Public and Commercial Services Union (PCS) employed by Interserve at the Foreign and Commonwealth Office (FCO) who will embark on a month of strike action throughout February; notes that this dispute has been ongoing since March 2019 over issues including pay, trade union recognition, removal of working hours for cleaners and sick pay; further notes that the last strike ballot in October returned a 100 per cent vote in support of further strike action on an 82.5 per cent turnout; commends the efforts of PCS in their attempts throughout this dispute to seek resolution via the conciliator, ACAS, and their calls for urgent meetings with the employer and Secretary of State for Foreign and Commonwealth Affairs during the most recent ballot period; is concerned that treatment of staff employed by Interserve at the FCO has worsened in recent months, with those involved in the dispute excluded from festive activities and attempts being made by their employer to close their designated break room; notes that no worker takes the decision to go on strike lightly, and therefore calls on the Secretary of State to intervene urgently to bring an end to this dispute and ensure all workers in Government are treated with dignity and respect.

126 Solidarity with GMB and Unison workers at Luton & Dunstable Hospital

Tabled: 30/01/20 Signatories: 1

That this House supports GMB and Unison workers at Luton & Dunstable Hospital taking action against the outsourcing of non-clinical staff; notes that outsourcing in cleaning and catering risks not only higher rates of hospital-acquired infections but brings worse conditions for staff; further notes that the previous outsourced contract led to the creation of a three-tiered workforce that has undermined the working conditions of staff; further notes that Engie planned to cut wages for staff by 20 per cent, or £2000 a year; urges managers at Luton & Dunstable Hospital to take the concerns of the public and hospital workers seriously and consider bringing services back in-house; and supports those workers fighting for better pay, better conditions and the benefits of keeping the NHS public.

128 Commemoration of the 30th year of the displacement of Hindus from the valley of Kashmir in India

Tabled: 30/01/20 Signatories: 1

Bob Blackman

That this House commemorates with deep sadness the 30th anniversary of the attack in January 1990 by cross-border Islamic militants on the population of Jammu and Kashmir; expresses its condolences to the families and friends of all those who were killed, raped and injured in that massacre and condemns the desecration of the holiest sites in Jammu and Kashmir; further expresses its concern that Kashmiris who fled to save life and limb have still not secured justice for the atrocities committed against them; deplores those sponsoring cross-border terrorist attacks and demands they cease immediately; notes that the international principle of the responsibility to protect obliges individual states and the international community to take effective measures to prevent the commission of genocide and crimes against humanity as seen against the Kashmiri Hindu community; and commends the resilience and courage shown by the surviving members of Kashmiri Hindu community that did not resort to picking up guns but instead pursued education and aspiration and today make a huge contribution to the UK.

129 Universal credit and state pension transition

Tabled: 30/01/20 Signatories: 1

Mark Tami

This House notes that a claimant of universal credit that is approaching state pension age may be subject to nine weeks without income between the end of universal credit and the first payment of the state pension if they are not eligible for pension credit; further notes that that period includes up to thirty days in which no benefit is payable and that the final universal credit assessment period can be reduced and paid on a pro-rata basis; and calls on the Government to ensure that universal credit is payable up to the day before the state pension is paid, regardless of whether pension credit is subsequently payable.

81 Providing financial restitution to 1950s women

Tabled: 20/01/20 Signatories: 42

Paula Barker

Rachel Hopkins

Allan Dorans

Jonathan Edwards

Ben Lake

Olivia Blake

Mark TamiSarah OlneySir Edward Davey

That this House welcomes the positive interventions from many hon. Members from across the House on behalf of women born in the 1950s who have lost their pensions; welcomes the equalisation of retirement ages between women and men; recalls that women born in the 1950s were subject to discriminatory employment and pension laws; recognises that this included being excluded from some pensions schemes; recognises that this had the negative effect for them of losing the opportunity to have the same level of pension as their partner or spouse; further recognises that this has had the consequence of women in this position never being able to have equal pensions to men; further notes that this has negatively and profoundly impacted on them including increased poverty, deteriorating health and homelessness; notes that at least 3.8 million women have been impacted by the loss of their pensions from the age of 60 in three separate age hikes; and calls on the Government to enact a temporary special measure as permitted by international law to provide restitution to women born in the 1950s who have lost their pensions from the age of 60 because of the impact of the rise in retirement age.

Gordon Henderson

Drew Hendry

Claudia Webbe

Jim ShannonChris StephensJonathan EdwardsBob BlackmanKenny MacAskill
