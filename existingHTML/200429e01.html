Early Day Motions tabled on Wednesday 29 April 2020

Early Day Motions (EDMs) are motions for which no days have been fixed.

The number of signatories includes all members who have added their names in support of the Early Day Motion (EDM), including the Member in charge of the Motion.

EDMs and added names are also published on the EDM database at www.parliament.uk/edm

[R] Indicates that a relevant interest has been declared.

New EDMs

402 Support for the port of Holyhead during the covid-19 outbreak

Tabled: 29/04/20 Signatories: 1

Hywel Williams

That this House is concerned that despite a sharp drop in trade due to the covid-19 pandemic, the Government’s £17 million support package for ferry services does not include the Holyhead – Dublin route; notes that as the UK’s second roll on roll off port Holyhead is critical for the Welsh and UK economies with in excess of a million vehicle movements a year; recalls that it is also a vital route for the Northern Ireland economy; further notes that the port of Holyhead sustains more than 400 jobs and many hundreds more through its supply chain; and urges the Government to provide financial support to the port of Holyhead as it does to other UK ports, and to work with both the Welsh and Irish governments to safeguard its future beyond the covid-19 crisis.

403 Covid-19 in Welsh prisons

Tabled: 29/04/20 Signatories: 1

Liz Saville Roberts

That this House is alarmed that 25 per cent of confirmed covid-19 cases in prisons in England and Wales have been in Welsh facilities, despite only having 6 per cent of the prison population; is concerned that the number of people held in Welsh prisons reached its highest ever level in March 2020 and that HMP Swansea was the most overcrowded prison in England and Wales; is further concerned that as of 28 April, only thirty-three out of 4,000 prisoners eligible for temporary release to help tackle covid-19 have so far been released, and that the number of prisoners who will be temporarily released from Welsh establishments has not been finalised; calls on the Ministry of Justice to urgently release more of those eligible for temporary release in order to stop the rapid spread of the virus in our prison system; and stresses that those being released must pose no risk to the public and must get tested for covid-19 prior to release.

404 Protecting maritime employment

Tabled: 29/04/20 Signatories: 2

Liz Saville Roberts

Ben Lake

That this House notes with concern the unprecedented impact of covid-19 on the maritime economy; supports the RMT trade union’s proposals for protecting UK maritime workers from the long-term economic effects of the coronavirus crisis; calls for no compulsory redundancies of UK seafarers, dock or port workers during or after the covid-19 crisis; urges the Government to ensure that furlough and cash flow support payments only go to shipping companies that state their commitment to long term investment in employment and training for UK seafarers; demands reform of the UK Ship Register and the end Red Ensign Group to restore the link with employment for domestic Ratings and Officers in order to limit the possibility of companies and individuals to avoid tax liabilities and use low-cost labour; requests increased public funding for training and developing UK Ratings; and urges the Government to implement these necessary measures to build a resilient and stable maritime future.

That this House celebrates the centenary of the birth of Edwin Morgan, considered one of Scotland's greatest poets, who was born on 27 April 1920; recognises that he was named the first Scots Makar in 2004, first Glasgow Poet Laureate inn 1999, and recipient of the Queen's Gold Medal for Poetry in 2000; notes that he matriculated at the University of Glasgow in 1937, his studies interrupted by World War Two during which he served as a non-combatant conscientious objector, graduating in 1947 before teaching there his entire working life, being appointed titular Professor of English in 1975; recognises that he published 25 collections of poetry, translated hundreds of poems and wrote plays which were forward-thinking, internationalist and intergalactic and continue to be published, produced, taught and celebrated around the world; appreciates that Glasgow and Scotland lay at the heart of his accessible, engaging and often humorous work, and that his writing about his experience as a gay man had a significant impact on social attitudes and political change in Scotland; acknowledges his continuing legacy through the Edwin Morgan Trust and Poetry Award for young poets in Scotland; supports the Trust's #EdwinMorgan100 celebrations and looks forward to its physical programme of events taking place From Glasgow to Saturn once it is safe to do so; and remembers his words written for the opening of the Scottish Parliament building in 2004, We give you our deepest dearest wish to govern well, don’t say we have no mandate to be so bold.
